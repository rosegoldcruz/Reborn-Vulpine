Structural Moat — Tariff-Protected Pricing
In October 2025, Section 232 tariffs of 25% took effect on Chinese-origin kitchen cabinets (HTS 9403.40, 9403.90). The rate is scheduled to step to 50% on January 1, 2027. Combined with existing Section 301 duties and AD/CVD orders, Chinese-manufactured cabinets now carry effective duty rates exceeding 70%. Vietnam-origin cabinets, the largest import source after China, face combined rates of approximately 35%.
Impress Cabinetry operates factory-direct with US warehouse operations in California. Competitors sourcing Chinese or Vietnamese product are absorbing 25-70%+ landed-cost increases or passing them directly to customers. Our pricing structure is not exposed to those tariff stacks.

The first-message value prop should reduce coordination burden and quoting friction before anything else. Labor-constrained contractors respond to operational relief, not innovation stories.
Lead with price and speed. Introduce tech after immediate value is proven. The first message should emphasize reliable cabinet execution with stronger economics. The second message can position tech as a practical system for protecting inbound leads and improving conversion.
1
List Building — targeted, not broad
Pull targets from state registry data and regional trade directories. Enrich with website quality, customer ratings, form quality, and whether they visibly market kitchen/bath work. Goal: a ranked list of owner-operators and small GCs who actually buy cabinets.
2
First-Message Offer — short, operational, low-commitment
Under 100 words. Gong research shows pitching the solution too early cuts reply rates by up to 57% (Gong.io, 2025). Use an interest-based CTA, not a call ask. Proven opener: "Hey [Name] — we provide wholesale cabinet pricing in Phoenix with a lean-margin model and fast quote turnaround. If you have one live kitchen or investor turn that needs a sharper number, send it over and I'll price it clean with alternates. If useful, we can also tighten your website/CRM after the first job."
3
Tripwire Transaction — fast and undeniable
24-hour cabinet quote, a 3-option package on one active project, or a missed-call teardown for contractors whose websites clearly underperform. Don't make the first deal large. Make it fast and undeniable. Speed is the trust mechanism here, not price alone.
4
Recurring-Account Conversion — lock them in operationally
After the first job: saved spec library, standing quote template, 30-day pricing sheet on common lines, same-day revision policy. Then the tech layer becomes practical: CRM stages, missed-call text-back, testimonial automation, pipeline dashboards, and call handling support. 87% of contractors expect AI-driven transformation — only 19% have adapted workflows. That's the second sale.

Section 06

Risk Mitigation

Every operational and market risk identified with specific mitigations built into the plan.

High
Single supplier concentration
Primary wholesale supplier outage stops Stream 1. Mitigation: pre-vet 2 backup RTA suppliers with pricing and lead times confirmed before month 2.
High
Subcontractor capacity limit
One licensed sub with reliability questions. Mitigation: build bench of 3+ refacing crews by month 3, pay top-of-market per job to lock loyalty before the pipeline fills.
Medium
Phoenix market softening
Flip market slows → Stream 1 dips. Mitigation: refacing demand is more mortgage-rate resilient. Tilt mix toward Stream 2 when flip pipeline softens.
Medium
TPT / MRRA misclassification
Refacing classified incorrectly = sales tax exposure. Mitigation: annual review with AZ-licensed CPA, clear job-type tagging in CRM from day one.
Medium
Lead cost inflation
CAC erodes margin as Phoenix ad competition increases. Mitigation: layer referral, contractor partner, and direct MFU outreach alongside paid. Don't depend on Google alone.
Low
Door manufacturer delays
Texas CNC partner slips on 7-day promise. Mitigation: maintain 7-day buffer in customer commitments, pre-qualify secondary Texas door vendor before month 2.
