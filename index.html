Customer Acquisition Strategy

One wedge-based system. Three buyer types. Different front doors — same relationship graph.

Strategic thesis: Don't market this as three unrelated businesses. Run one wedge-based acquisition system with different front doors. For contractors: cabinet economics first, tech second, referrals third. For multifamily: fast refacing for turns, supply for heavier scopes, tech as the back-office stickiness layer. For homeowners: refacing entry, full replacement upsell. One relationship unlocks multiple monetization routes.

Timing advantage: 92% of AZ construction firms can't find workers. 45% are delaying projects because of it. The first-message value prop should reduce coordination burden and quoting friction before anything else. Labor-constrained contractors respond to operational relief, not innovation stories.
Lead with price + speed. Lead with tech only when the contractor's digital pain is painfully obvious from the outside. The first thing a GC wants to hear is "I can cover cabinet scope fast, cleanly, below the fat-margin guys." The second thing — once proven — is "I can also stop you from dropping leads."
1
List Building — targeted, not broad
Pull targets from ROC search + NARI, HBACA, ABA, Arizona AGC member directories. Enrich with website quality, review count, form quality, and whether they visibly market kitchen/bath work. Goal: a ranked list of owner-operators and small GCs who actually buy cabinets.
2
First-Message Offer — short, operational, low-commitment
Under 100 words. Gong research: pitching the solution too early cuts reply rates by up to 57%. Use an interest-based CTA, not a call ask. Proven opener: "Hey [Name] — we're pricing wholesale cabinets in Phoenix intentionally below the fat-margin players. If you've got one live kitchen or investor turn that needs a sharper number, send it over and I'll price it clean with alternates. If useful, we can also tighten your website/CRM after the first job."
3
Tripwire Transaction — fast and undeniable
24-hour cabinet quote, a 3-option package on one active project, or a missed-call teardown for contractors whose websites clearly underperform. Don't make the first deal large. Make it fast and undeniable. Speed is the trust mechanism here, not price alone.
4
Recurring-Account Conversion — lock them in operationally
After the first job: saved spec library, standing quote template, 30-day pricing sheet on common lines, same-day revision policy. Then the tech layer becomes practical: CRM stages, missed-call text-back, review automation, pipeline dashboards, AI call handling. 87% of contractors expect AI-driven transformation — only 19% have adapted workflows. That's the second sale.
The PM motion starts from a bought list, not vague prospecting. AMA membership list: $150 for phone numbers + addresses of member management companies. Enrich on LinkedIn by title: regional property manager, asset manager, portfolio manager, maintenance supervisor, renovation/project lead.

Section 06

Risk Mitigation

Every operational and market risk identified with specific mitigations built into the plan.

High
Single supplier concentration
Impress Cabinetry outage stops Stream 1. Mitigation: pre-vet 2 backup RTA suppliers with pricing and lead times confirmed before month 2.
High
Subcontractor capacity limit
One licensed sub with reliability questions. Mitigation: build bench of 3+ refacing crews by month 3, pay top-of-market per job to lock loyalty before the pipeline fills.
Medium
Phoenix market softening
Flip market slows → Stream 1 dips. Mitigation: refacing demand is more mortgage-rate resilient. Tilt mix toward Stream 2 when flip pipeline softens.
Medium
TPT / MRRA misclassification
Refacing classified incorrectly = sales tax exposure. Mitigation: annual review with AZ-licensed CPA, clear job-type tagging in CRM from day one.
Medium
Lead cost inflation
CAC erodes margin as Phoenix ad competition increases. Mitigation: layer referral, contractor partner, and direct MFU outreach alongside paid. Don't depend on Google alone.
Low
Door manufacturer delays
Texas CNC partner slips on 7-day promise. Mitigation: maintain 7-day buffer in customer commitments, pre-qualify secondary Texas door vendor before month 2.
